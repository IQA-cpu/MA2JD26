Section 03

Formula Summary

Every formula you need for MA2 Managing Costs and Finances, grouped by syllabus part. These are the ones worth committing to memory before the exam.

PART A

Management Information

▶

High–low method: variable cost per unit

(Cost at high level − Cost at low level) ÷ (Units at high − Units at low)

Total cost (cost equation)

Fixed cost + (Variable cost per unit × Activity level)

Percentage variance

(Actual − Budget) ÷ Budget × 100

PART B

Cost Recording

▶

Economic order quantity (EOQ)

√( 2 × Co × D ÷ Ch )

Co = cost per order · D = annual demand · Ch = holding cost per unit per year

Reorder level

Maximum usage × Maximum lead time

Minimum inventory level

Reorder level − (Average usage × Average lead time)

Maximum inventory level

Reorder level + Reorder qty − (Minimum usage × Minimum lead time)

Labour turnover rate

(Replacements ÷ Average number of employees) × 100

Labour efficiency ratio

(Expected hours for output ÷ Actual hours worked) × 100

Capacity ratio

(Actual hours worked ÷ Budgeted hours) × 100

Production volume (activity) ratio

(Expected hours for output ÷ Budgeted hours) × 100

Idle time ratio

(Idle hours ÷ Total hours) × 100

PART C

Costing Techniques

▶

Overhead absorption rate (OAR)

Budgeted overheads ÷ Budgeted activity level

Over / (under) absorption

Overhead absorbed − Overhead actually incurred

Contribution

Sales − Variable costs

Contribution per unit

Selling price per unit − Variable cost per unit

Profit (marginal costing)

Total contribution − Fixed costs

Process costing: cost per equivalent unit

Total costs ÷ Total equivalent units

Batch costing: cost per unit

Total batch cost ÷ Number of units in batch

PART D

Decision Making

▶

Contribution to sales (C/S) ratio

(Contribution ÷ Sales) × 100

Breakeven point (units)

Fixed costs ÷ Contribution per unit

Breakeven point (revenue)

Fixed costs ÷ C/S ratio

Units for target profit

(Fixed costs + Target profit) ÷ Contribution per unit

Margin of safety (%)

(Budgeted sales − Breakeven sales) ÷ Budgeted sales × 100

Accounting rate of return (ARR)

(Average annual profit ÷ Average investment) × 100

Average investment

(Initial investment + Residual value) ÷ 2

Discount factor / present value

DF = 1 ÷ (1 + r)n  ·  PV = Cash flow × DF

r = discount rate · n = number of periods

Net present value (NPV)

Present value of inflows − Initial investment

PART E

Cash Management

▶

Simple interest

Principal × Rate × Number of periods

Compound interest (future value)

Principal × (1 + r)n

Cash operating cycle

Inventory days + Receivables days − Payables days

Inventory holding period

(Average inventory ÷ Cost of sales) × 365

Receivables collection period

(Receivables ÷ Credit sales) × 365

Payables payment period

(Payables ÷ Credit purchases) × 365

Closing cash balance

Opening balance + Receipts − Payments

Expected value (EV)

Σ ( Probability × Outcome )
